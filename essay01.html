Essay 01 - Changing Relationships: Friends, Enemies, Allies, Love Interests

An Introduction

I would like to begin this by saying that my defintion of relationships has changed over time. I think everyone can agree that their definition of relationships have changed over time as well. In elementary school, we say that the people that we talk to the most are our friends and the ones that are mean to us are our enemies. We don't ever think about making allies as we lived with a black and white mentality of what's right and wrong. And as far we're concerned, our love interests tend to be shallow. We fall for those who appear to be the most physically attractive.
Beyond elementary school, say our teenage years (average ages of 13 - 17), these definitions change. We consider making allies for temporary situations - perhaps for group projects and that's it. Our love interests are a bit more complex, say that they're the only person that can make us laugh or smile or whatnot. But love is certainly not that deep. However, the definition of friendship becomes more complex. As we get older, we tend to hold our friends to certain ideals and certain expectations. If you're social, you expect your friends to be more social. If you're more introverted, you expect your friends to respect your space. In this environment, it's no longer about the people you like talking the most, it's about who can help you be your best self. Your enemies on the other hand, tend to be more complicated. Sometimes they tend to be opposing the values that you hold to be important, rather than just badmouthing your presence. Or maybe your enemies still tend to hate you, but far more worse and for far more superficial reasons like your race or religion or sexual orientation or economic status or whatever core identity that makes you, you.
In college, these dynamics are more or less the same. But as one gets older, one tends to have more allies than friends. It's not clear when that distinction is made but it becomes more noticable over time. What does remain a constant though is how our perception of love becomes more complex. When we mature as young adults, we begin to fall in love for more genuine reasons than similarities of personal interest.

Status of my personal relationships:

I would like to say that I have more allies than friends. For a long time, I thought I had more friends than allies. But as I get older, I realize that I'm just very good at talking to people. I'm very good at understanding most people and what makes them work. But I've also haven't had the luxury of community. I've always been someone who drifts about from place to place, someone whose constantly learning and growing from the people around him.
I will say that most of my allies do come from places where I met my friends. I do have a good amount of allies from El Modena High School, Alpha Phi Omega, and Epic Movement. They are allies usually because we have an issue that units us together. When the issue is resolved, we don't have that reason to communicate with each other.
But I call a good amount of them allies because I know that on any given day that we will no longer be in touch with each other. Either due to the passing of time, a change of interests, or due to me cocking something up, people tend to leave my life.

What makes a close friend?

For me, close friends are those who I hold an invested interest in them because they tend to surprise me in one way or another. And this element of surprise is what keeps me grounded in reality. Because I due tend to think that I'm better than mainstream society. But I do need people who are better than me in certain aspects to keep me grounded just enough to remind me that I'm still human at the end of the day.
