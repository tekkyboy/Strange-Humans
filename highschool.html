The High School era (Fall 2015 - Spring 2018)

High school era spanned three years of my life, from the seasons of Fall 2015 to Spring 2018. It was during these three years where I learned to become more comfortable talking to lots of different people. Never really had a fixed friend group, always kept on bouncing between different people over the years. But in the end, it was worth it for the experience. The many humans that I have met over these years are listed down below, separated by the year as to when I met them as opposed to when I became friends with them. I would say that I became friends with all of my friends from high school after high school. So to me, it makes sense to list when I met them first.

About High School Me:

High School Kylan at his finest.

During my first year of high school (Fall 2014 - Spring 2015), I started off as a really quiet and shy teenage boy with severe social anxiety talking to people. But that soon started to change in our high school's AP World History class, where I was known for my many memetic quotes and speeches. It was that class that enabled me to be my best teenage self - a comedian and clown who often rebelled against authoritative figures and the status quo, becoming popular with my peers by derailing class time by going off on massive tangents, and involving classmates in my many side projects and social experiments. This popularity eclipsed in me becoming the Prom King of our graduating class.

About my High School friendships:

My friends during this time were a wide array of individuals localized mostly within our high school's AP community. All of my high school friends, I should say, are intelligent and open-minded people. I say this because collectively, all of my high school friends were interested in learning about who I was as an individual and why I acted the way I acted during classtime and outside of it. I would also like to say that most of my high school friends are loyal to one another. Everyone is genuinely supportive of one another, regardless of their social status, economic status, ethnicity, and so on.
